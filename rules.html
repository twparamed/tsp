組織章程

TSP Rules

第一章 總則

第一條 本會名稱為社團法人台灣醫療救護學會（以下簡稱本會）。

第二條 本會為依法設立、非以營利為目的之社會團體，以推動台灣醫療救護成為法定醫療專業，健全台灣醫療救護教育體系，並提升台灣醫療救護教學研究能量為宗旨。

第三條 本會以全國行政區域為組織區域。

第四條 本會會址設於主管機關所在地區，並得報經主管機關核准設分支機構。前項分支機構組織簡則由理事會擬訂，報請主管機關核准後行之。會址及分支機構之地址於設置及變更時應函報主管機關核備。

第五條 本會之任務如下：

  1. 推動台灣醫療救護教育體系，發展醫療救護相關科系，提升整體醫療救護職能， 使台灣醫療救護具有專業培育與執業環境。
  2. 推動台灣醫療救護教育與救護人員認證機制。
  3. 協助研擬國內醫療救護訓練、繼續教育訓練之內容。
  4. 舉辦及接受政府委託辦理教育訓練課程與研討會，提升台灣醫療救護研究風氣， 並且增加與國際醫療救護交流機會。
  5. 透過期刊發表與產官學合作方式提升台灣醫療救護研究發展能量。
  6. 以實證基礎協助訂定醫療救護準則。

第六條 本會之主管機關為內政部，目的事業主管機關依章程所訂宗旨及任務，主要為衛生福利部，其目的事業應受各該事業主管機關之指導、監督。

第二章 會員

第七條 本會會員分下列 6 種：

  1. 個人會員：認同本會宗旨，具緊急醫療救護法所稱緊急醫療救護人員資格，填具入會申請書，經理事會通過，並繳納會費後，為個人會員。
  2. 團體會員：凡贊同本會宗旨且與緊急醫療救護業務相關之立案機構或團體，填具入會申請書，經理事會通過，並繳納會費後，為團體會員，團體會員推派代表 1 人，以行使會員權利。
  3. 學生會員：認同本會宗旨，年滿 20 歲且具緊急救護技術員資格且就讀各級學校之在學學生(不含在職進修學生)，經理事會通過，並繳納會費後，為學生會員。
  4. 贊助會員：認同本會宗旨，贊助本會工作之團體或個人，經理事會通過，並繳納會費後，為贊助會員。
  5. 榮譽會員：從事推動本學會或醫療救護發展有功人員，經本會理監事會通過者。
  6. 相關會員：贊同本會宗旨不具個人會員資格者，填具入會申請書，經理事會通過，並繳納會費後成為相關會員。

第八條 會員（會員代表）有表決權、選舉權、被選舉權與罷免權。每一會員（會員代表）為一權。團體會員、相關會員、贊助會員、榮譽會員無前項權利。

第九條 會員有遵守本會章程、決議及繳納會費之義務。為利組織運作，會員未繳納會費者，不得享有會員權利，連續二年未繳納會費者，視為自動退會。會員經出會、退會或停權處分，應提於會員大會審議通過，會員如欲申請復會或復權時，除有正當理由經理事會審核通過者外，應繳清前所積欠之會費。

第十條 會員（會員代表）有違反法令，章程或不遵守會員大會決議時，得經理事會決議，予以警告或停權處分，其危害團體情節重大者，得經會員大會決議予以除名。

第十一條 會員有下列情事之一者，為出會：

  1. 喪失會員資格者。
  2. 經會員大會決議除名者。

第十二條 會員得以書面敘明理由向本會聲明退會。

第三章 組織及職權

第十三條 本會以會員大會為最高權力機構。會員（會員代表）人數超過三百人以上時得分區比例選出會員代表，再召開會員代表大會，行使會員大會職權。會員代表任期 3 年，其名額及選舉辦法由理事會擬訂，報請主管機關核備後行之。
註：會員代表任期應配合理監事任期訂之。

第十四條 會員（會員代表）大會之職權如下：

  1. 訂定與變更章程。
  2. 選舉及罷免理事、監事。
  3. 議決入會費、常年會費、事業費及會員捐款之數額及方式。
  4. 議決年度工作計畫、報告及預算、決算。
  5. 議決會員（會員代表）之除名處分。
  6. 議決財產之處分。
  7. 議決本會之解散。
  8. 議決與會員權利義務有關之其他重大事項。前項第八款重大事項之範圍由理事會定之。

第十五條 本會置理事 15 人、監事 5 人，由會員（會員代表）選舉之，分別成立理事會、監事會。選舉前項理事、監事時，依計票情形得同時選出候補理事 3 人，候補監事 1 人，遇理事、監事出缺時，分別依序遞補之。理事會得提出下屆理事、監事候選人參考名單。理事、監事得採用通訊選舉。通訊選舉辦法由理事會通過，報請主管機關核備後行之。

第十六條 理事會之職權如下：

  1. 審定會員（會員代表）之資格。
  2. 選舉及罷免常務理事、理事長、副理事長。
  3. 議決理事、常務理事、副理事長及理事長之辭職。
  4. 聘免工作人員。
  5. 擬訂年度工作計畫、報告及預算、決算。
  6. 其他應執行事項。

第十七條 理事會置常務理事 5 人，由理事互選之，並由理事就常務理事中選舉 1 人為理事長， 副理事長 2 人，其選任方式與理事長同。理事長對內綜理督導會務，對外代表本會，並擔任會員大會、理事會主席。理事長因事不能執行職務時，應指定副理事長 1 人代理之，未指定或不能指定時，由常務理事互推 1 人代理之。理事長、副理事長、常務理事出缺時，應於 1 個月內補選之。

第十八條 監事會之職權如下：

  1. 監察理事會工作之執行。
  2. 審核年度決算。
  3. 選舉及罷免常務監事。
  4. 議決監事及常務監事之辭職。
  5. 其他應監察事項。

第十九條 監事會置常務監事 1 人，由監事互選之，監察日常會務，並擔任監事會主席。常務監事因事不能執行職務時，應指定監事 1 人代理之，未指定或不能指定時，由監事互推 1 人代理之。監事會主席（常務監事）出缺時應於 1 個月內補選之。

第二十條 理事、監事均為無給職，任期 3 年，連選得連任。理事長之連任，以 1 次為限。理事、監事之任期自召開本屆第 1 次理事會之日起計算。

第二十一條 理事、監事有下列情事之一者，應即解任：

  1. 喪失會員（會員代表）資格者。
  2. 因故辭職經理事會或監事會決議通過者。
  3. 被罷免或撤免者。
  4. 受停權處分期間逾任期二分之一者。

第二十二條 本會置秘書長 1 人，承理事長之命處理本會事務，其他工作人員若干人，由理事長提名經理事會通過聘免之，並報主管機關備查。前項工作人員不得由理事監事擔任。工作人員權責及分層負責事項由理事會另定之。

第二十三條 本會得設各種委員會、小組或其他內部作業組織，其組織簡則經理事會通過後施行，變更時亦同。

第二十四條 本會得由理事會聘請名譽理事長 1 人，名譽理事、顧問若干人，其聘期與理事、監事之任期同。

第四章 會議

第二十五條 會員（會員代表）大會分定期會議與臨時會議二種，由理事長召集，召集時除緊急事故之臨時會議外應於 15 日前以書面通知之。定期會議每年召開 1 次，臨時會議於理事會認為必要，或經會員（會員代表）五分之一以上之請求，或監事會函請召集時召開之。本會辦理法人登記後，臨時會議經會員（會員代表）十分之一以上之請求召開之。

第二十六條 會員（會員代表）不能親自出席會員大會時，得以書面委託其他會員（會員代表）代理，每一會員（會員代表）以代理 1 人為限。

第二十七條 會員（會員代表）大會之決議，以會員（會員代表）過半數之出席，出席人數較多數之同意行之。但下列事項之決議以出席人數三分之二以上同意行之。

  1. 章程之訂定與變更。
  2. 會員（會員代表）之除名。
  3. 理事、監事之罷免。
  4. 財產之處分。
  5. 本會之解散。
  6. 其他與會員權利義務有關之重大事項。

本會辦理法人登記後，章程之變更以出席人數四分之三以上之同意或全體會員三分之二以上書面之同意行之。本會之解散，得隨時以全體會員三分之二以上之可決議解散之。

第二十八條 理事會每 6 個月召開 1 次，監事會每 6 個月召開 1 次，必要時得召開聯席會議或臨時會議。前項會議召集時除臨時會議外，應於 7 日前以書面通知，會議之決議，各以理事、監事過半數之出席，出席人數較多數之同意行之。

第二十九條 理事應出席理事會議，監事應出席監事會議，理事會、監事會不得委託出席：理事、監事連續 2 次無故缺席理事會、監事會者，視同辭職。

第五章 經費及會計

第三十條 本會經費來源如下：

  1. 入會費：於會員入會時繳納。
    • 個人會員：新台幣 2,000 元。
    • 團體會員：新台幣 10,000 元。
    • 學生會員：新台幣 1,000 元。
    • 贊助會員：新台幣 10,000 元。
    • 榮譽會員：免繳會費。
    • 相關會員：新台幣 1,000 元。
  2. 常年會費：個人、學生、相關會員皆新台幣 1,000 元，團體會員、贊助會員新台幣 10,000 元，榮譽會員免繳年費。
  3. 事業費。
  4. 會員捐款。
  5. 委託收益。
  6. 基金及其孳息。
  7. 其他收入。

第三十一條 本會會計年度以曆年為準，自每年 1 月 1 日起至 12 月 31 日止。

第三十二條 本會每年於會計年度開始前 2 個月由理事會編造年度工作計畫、收支預算表、員工待遇表，提會員大會通過（會員大會因故未能如期召開者，先提理監事聯席會議通過），於會計年度開始前報主管機關核備。並於會計年度終了後二個月內由理事會編造年度工作報告、收支決算表、現金出納表、資產負債表、財產目錄及基金收支表，送監事會審核後，造具審核意見書送還理事會，提會員大會通過，於 3 月底前報主管機關核備（會員大會未能如期召開者，先報主管機關）。

第三十三條 本會於解散後，剩餘財產歸屬所在地之地方自治團體或主管機關指定之機關團體所有。

第六章 附則

第三十四條 本章程未規定事項，悉依有關法令規定辦理。

第三十五條 本章程經會員（會員代表）大會通過，報經主管機關核備後施行，變更時亦同。

第三十六條 本章程經本會 107 年 7 月 13 日第 1 屆第 1 次會員大會通過。

Chapter 1: General Provisions

Article 1 The name of this association shall be the Taiwan Society of Paramedicine (hereinafter referred to as the Society).

Article 2 The Society is a legally established, non-profit social organization. Its missions are to promote paramedicine as a statutory medical profession in Taiwan, to improve the education system for paramedicine, and to enhance the capacity for paramedicine teaching and research.

Article 3 The organizational region of the Society shall be the national administrative region of Taiwan.

Article 4 The headquarters of the Society shall be located in the region where the competent authority is situated, and branch offices may be established upon approval by the competent authority. The organizational regulations for such branch offices shall be drafted by the Board of Directors and implemented after approval by the competent authority. Any establishment or change of address for the headquarters and branch offices shall be reported to the competent authority for recordation.

Article 5 The tasks of the Society are as follows:

  1. To promote the paramedicine education system, develop paramedicine-related academic departments, elevate overall occupational competencies, and ensure a professional training and practice environment for paramedicine in Taiwan.
  2. To promote paramedicine education and the certification mechanism for paramedics.
  3. To assist in formulating the content of domestic paramedicine training and continuing medical education (CME).
  4. To organize and accept government commissions to conduct training courses and symposiums, elevate the research atmosphere of paramedicine in Taiwan, and increase opportunities for international paramedicine exchange.
  5. To enhance the research and development capacity of paramedicine in Taiwan through journal publications and industry-government-academia collaborations.
  6. To assist in establishing evidence-based paramedicine guidelines.

Article 6 The competent authority of the Society is the Ministry of the Interior. The target enterprise competent authority, based on the missions and tasks outlined in these Articles, is primarily the Ministry of Health and Welfare. The target enterprises of the Society shall be subject to the guidance and supervision of their respective competent authorities.

Chapter 2: Membership

Article 7 The members of the Society are classified into the following 6 categories:

  1. Individual Members: Individuals who identify with the missions of the Society, possess the qualifications of emergency medical personnel as defined by the Emergency Medical Services Act, complete the membership application, are approved by the Board of Directors, and have paid the membership dues.
  2. Group Members: Registered institutions or organizations that support the missions of the Society and are related to emergency medical services. Upon completing the application, receiving Board approval, and paying dues, they become Group Members and shall appoint 1 representative to exercise membership rights.
  3. Student Members: Individuals who identify with the missions of the Society, are at least 20 years old, possess Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) certification, and are currently enrolled as students in educational institutions at any level (excluding on-the-job continuing education students), upon Board approval and payment of dues.
  4. Sponsoring Members: Organizations or individuals who identify with the missions of the Society and sponsor its work, upon Board approval and payment of dues.
  5. Honorary Members: Individuals who have made significant contributions to the promotion of the Society or the development of paramedicine, upon approval by the Board of Directors and Supervisors.
  6. Associate Members: Individuals who support the missions of the Society but do not meet the qualifications for Individual Membership, upon completing the application, receiving Board approval, and paying dues.

Article 8 Members (or Member Representatives) shall have the right to vote, elect, be elected, and recall. Each Member (or Member Representative) holds one vote. Group Members, Associate Members, Sponsoring Members, and Honorary Members do not possess the aforementioned rights.

Article 9 Members have the obligation to abide by the Articles of Association and resolutions of the Society, and to pay membership dues. To facilitate organizational operations, members who fail to pay dues shall not enjoy membership rights; those who fail to pay dues for two consecutive years shall be deemed to have voluntarily withdrawn. Members who withdraw, are expelled, or are suspended shall have their cases reviewed and approved by the General Assembly. When seeking reinstatement of membership or rights, unless there is a justified reason approved by the Board of Directors, all outstanding dues must be paid in full.

Article 10 Members (or Member Representatives) who violate laws, these Articles, or fail to comply with General Assembly resolutions may be issued a warning or suspended by a resolution of the Board of Directors. For severe violations harming the Society, they may be expelled by a resolution of the General Assembly.

Article 11 A member shall be deemed to have withdrawn from the Society under any of the following circumstances:

  1. Loss of membership qualifications.
  2. Expulsion by resolution of the General Assembly.

Article 12 Members may voluntarily withdraw from the Society by stating their reasons in writing.

Chapter 3: Organization and Authority

Article 13 The General Assembly is the highest authority of the Society. When the number of members (or Member Representatives) exceeds 300, Member Representatives may be elected proportionally by district to convene a Member Representative Assembly to exercise the powers of the General Assembly. The term of Member Representatives is 3 years; the quota and election regulations shall be drafted by the Board of Directors and implemented after reporting to the competent authority for recordation.
Note: The term of Member Representatives shall align with the term of the Directors and Supervisors.

Article 14 The authority of the General Assembly (or Member Representative Assembly) is as follows:

  1. To establish and amend the Articles of Association.
  2. To elect and recall Directors and Supervisors.
  3. To resolve on the amount and method of payment for initiation fees, annual dues, enterprise fees, and member donations.
  4. To resolve on annual work plans, reports, budgets, and final accounts.
  5. To resolve on the expulsion of members (or Member Representatives).
  6. To resolve on the disposal of property.
  7. To resolve on the dissolution of the Society.
  8. To resolve on other significant matters related to members' rights and obligations. The scope of significant matters in this subparagraph shall be determined by the Board of Directors.

Article 15 The Society shall have 15 Directors and 5 Supervisors, elected by the members (or Member Representatives), to form the Board of Directors and the Board of Supervisors, respectively. During the election, based on the vote count, 3 Alternate Directors and 1 Alternate Supervisor may be elected simultaneously to fill any vacancies in order. The Board of Directors may propose a reference list of candidates for the next term. Elections may be conducted via correspondence; the regulations for correspondence voting shall be approved by the Board of Directors and reported to the competent authority for recordation.

Article 16 The authority of the Board of Directors is as follows:

  1. To review and qualify members (or Member Representatives).
  2. To elect and recall Standing Directors, the President, and the Vice Presidents.
  3. To resolve on the resignation of Directors, Standing Directors, Vice Presidents, and the President.
  4. To appoint and dismiss staff members.
  5. To draft annual work plans, reports, budgets, and final accounts.
  6. Other matters requiring execution.

Article 17 The Board of Directors shall have 5 Standing Directors elected from among the Directors, and the Directors shall elect 1 President and 2 Vice Presidents from among the Standing Directors. The President oversees the internal affairs of the Society, represents the Society externally, and serves as the chairperson of the General Assembly and the Board of Directors. If the President is unable to perform their duties, they shall designate 1 Vice President to act on their behalf; if no designation is made or cannot be made, the Standing Directors shall elect one among themselves to act as a proxy. Vacancies for the President, Vice Presidents, or Standing Directors shall be filled via a by-election within 1 month.

Article 18 The authority of the Board of Supervisors is as follows:

  1. To supervise the execution of work by the Board of Directors.
  2. To audit the annual final accounts.
  3. To elect and recall the Standing Supervisor.
  4. To resolve on the resignation of Supervisors and the Standing Supervisor.
  5. Other matters requiring supervision.

Article 19 The Board of Supervisors shall have 1 Standing Supervisor, elected from among the Supervisors, to supervise daily affairs and serve as the chairperson of the Board of Supervisors. If the Standing Supervisor is unable to perform their duties, they shall designate 1 Supervisor to act on their behalf; if no designation is made or cannot be made, the Supervisors shall elect one among themselves to act as a proxy. A vacancy for the Chairperson of the Board of Supervisors (Standing Supervisor) shall be filled via a by-election within 1 month.

Article 20 Directors and Supervisors are unpaid positions with a term of 3 years and may be re-elected for consecutive terms. The President may only be re-elected for one consecutive term. The terms of Directors and Supervisors commence on the day the first Board of Directors meeting of the current term is convened.

Article 21 Directors or Supervisors shall be immediately dismissed under any of the following circumstances:

  1. Loss of member (or Member Representative) qualification.
  2. Resignation approved by a resolution of the Board of Directors or Board of Supervisors.
  3. Being recalled or dismissed.
  4. Being suspended for a period exceeding half of their term.

Article 22 The Society shall appoint 1 Secretary General to handle the Society's affairs under the President's instructions, and several other staff members. They shall be nominated by the President, appointed/dismissed upon approval by the Board of Directors, and reported to the competent authority for reference. The aforementioned staff may not concurrently serve as Directors or Supervisors. The responsibilities and hierarchical delegation of duties for staff shall be determined separately by the Board of Directors.

Article 23 The Society may establish various committees, working groups, or other internal operational organizations. Their organizational regulations shall be implemented after approval by the Board of Directors, and the same applies to any amendments.

Article 24 The Board of Directors may appoint 1 Honorary President and several Honorary Directors or Advisors. Their term of appointment shall match that of the current Directors and Supervisors.

Chapter 4: Meetings

Article 25 The General Assembly (or Member Representative Assembly) consists of Regular Meetings and Extraordinary Meetings, convened by the President. Except for Emergency Extraordinary Meetings, written notice must be given 15 days in advance. Regular Meetings are held once a year. Extraordinary Meetings are convened when the Board of Directors deems necessary, upon the request of at least one-fifth of the members, or upon a written request from the Board of Supervisors. After the Society's corporate registration, Extraordinary Meetings may be convened upon the request of at least one-tenth of the members.

Article 26 When a member (or Member Representative) cannot attend the General Assembly in person, they may authorize another member (or Member Representative) in writing to act as a proxy. Each member (or Member Representative) may only act as a proxy for 1 person.

Article 27 Resolutions of the General Assembly require the attendance of more than half of the members (or Member Representatives) and the consent of the majority of those present. However, resolutions on the following matters require the consent of at least two-thirds of attendees:

  1. Establishment and amendment of the Articles of Association.
  2. Expulsion of members (or Member Representatives).
  3. Recall of Directors or Supervisors.
  4. Disposal of property.
  5. Dissolution of the Society.
  6. Other significant matters related to members' rights and obligations.

After the Society's corporate registration, amendments to the Articles of Association require the consent of at least three-fourths of attendees or the written consent of at least two-thirds of all members. The dissolution of the Society may be resolved at any time by the affirmative vote of at least two-thirds of all members.

Article 28 The Board of Directors and the Board of Supervisors shall each meet once every 6 months. Joint meetings or extraordinary meetings may be convened when necessary. Except for extraordinary meetings, written notice must be given 7 days in advance. Resolutions require the attendance of more than half of the Directors or Supervisors and the consent of the majority of those present.

Article 29 Directors and Supervisors must attend their respective board meetings in person and may not authorize proxies. Any Director or Supervisor who is absent without a valid reason for 2 consecutive meetings shall be deemed to have resigned.

Chapter 5: Funding and Accounting

Article 30 The funding sources of the Society are as follows:

  1. Initiation fee: Paid upon joining.
    • Individual Members: NT$ 2,000.
    • Group Members: NT$ 10,000.
    • Student Members: NT$ 1,000.
    • Sponsoring Members: NT$ 10,000.
    • Honorary Members: Exempt.
    • Associate Members: NT$ 1,000.
  2. Annual dues: NT$ 1,000 for Individual, Student, and Associate Members; NT$ 10,000 for Group and Sponsoring Members; Honorary Members are exempt.
  3. Enterprise income.
  4. Member donations.
  5. Commissioned project revenues.
  6. Funds and their accrued interest.
  7. Other income.

Article 31 The fiscal year of the Society aligns with the calendar year, from January 1 to December 31.

Article 32 Two months before the start of each fiscal year, the Board of Directors shall prepare an annual work plan, budget of revenues and expenditures, and employee remuneration table, to be approved by the General Assembly (if the Assembly cannot be convened, it shall first be approved by a joint meeting of Directors and Supervisors) and reported to the competent authority before the fiscal year begins. Within two months after the end of the fiscal year, the Board of Directors shall prepare an annual work report, final statement of revenues and expenditures, cash flow statement, balance sheet, property inventory, and fund balance statement. These shall be audited by the Board of Supervisors, which will provide an audit opinion report to the Board of Directors, to be approved by the General Assembly and reported to the competent authority before the end of March (if the Assembly cannot be convened, it shall be reported to the competent authority first).

Article 33 Upon the dissolution of the Society, any remaining assets shall belong to the local autonomous body where the Society is located or an agency/organization designated by the competent authority.

Chapter 6: Supplementary Provisions

Article 34 Matters not stipulated in these Articles shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 35 These Articles of Association shall be implemented after approval by the General Assembly (or Member Representative Assembly) and reporting to the competent authority for recordation. The same applies to any amendments.

Article 36 These Articles of Association were approved at the 1st General Assembly of the 1st Term on July 13, 2018.
